Research

Our lab combines developmental biology, functional genetics, and genomics to understand how organisms adapt to extreme environments.

Eco-Evo-Devo of Annual Killifishes

Killifishes are emerging research models for evolution, development, behavior, cancer, developmental perturbations, and aging. Annual killifishes have dormant embryos capable of surviving seasonal habitat desiccation. This life history includes convergent evolution of rapid aging and embryonic dormancy across distantly related lineages.

Using annual and non-annual killifishes, we explore how environmental gradients shape development, life history, and phenotypic plasticity.

Approaches: ATAC-seq, single-nucleus RNA-seq, Whole Genome Resequencing

Developmental Genomics of Ancient Fishes

Many fishes descended from a common ancestor that experienced whole-genome duplication. Some slowly evolving “living fossil” fishes diverged before this duplication. By studying bowfin, gar, and lampreys we investigate deep homology of vertebrate developmental systems.

Slowly evolving genomes like gar and bowfin provide critical evolutionary bridges between humans and rapidly evolving teleost models.

Approaches: ATAC-seq, Comparative Genomics, Bioinformatics
